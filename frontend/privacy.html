Privacy Policy

Last updated: [DATE — update this each time you revise the policy]

Grace Community Church ("we", "us", "our") respects your privacy. This page explains, in plain language, what information we collect through this website, why we collect it, who can see it, and how you can ask us to change or remove it. Editor's note: this is a starting template, not legal advice — please have someone familiar with your local privacy laws review and adapt it before relying on it.

What We Collect

  • Prayer requests — the request text you write, your name (unless you submit anonymously), and optional contact information if you want follow-up.
  • "Connect With Us" / registration form — your name, and whichever contact details you provide (phone and/or email), your preferred contact method, and any notes you add.
  • Member accounts — your name, email, phone (optional), preferred contact method, and a securely hashed password (we never store or see your actual password).
  • Group messages — the content of messages you send in church groups you belong to.

Who Can See It

  • Prayer requests are private. Only our pastoral team (admins and group leaders) can view them. If you submit anonymously, your name and contact info are hidden even from that team.
  • Registration/follow-up submissions are visible only to admins and leaders, for the purpose of reaching out to you as you requested.
  • Member directory details (name, email, phone, contact preference) are visible only to admins and leaders — never to the general public, and never to other ordinary members.
  • Group messages are visible only to members of that specific group, plus admins/leaders.

Why We Collect It

We use this information to pray for you, follow up with visitors and members the way they've asked us to, run church communications and small groups, and organize events. We do not sell, rent, or share your information with advertisers or unrelated third parties.

How It's Stored

Data is stored in our church's own database, protected by password hashing, access controls, and standard web security practices. As with any online system, we take reasonable steps to protect your data but cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Choices

  • You can submit a prayer request anonymously if you'd rather not share your name.
  • You can ask us to correct, update, or delete your information at any time.
  • You can ask what information we hold about you.
  • You can unsubscribe from non-essential email communications at any time.

To make any of these requests, please contact us at privacy@example.com [Editor's note: replace with your church's real contact email].

Children

This site is intended for use by adults and older youth submitting information on their own behalf. If you believe a child has submitted personal information without appropriate parental involvement, please contact us and we will address it promptly.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time as the site changes. We'll update the "last updated" date above when we do.
